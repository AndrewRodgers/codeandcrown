Grants Explorer

Robert Wood Johnson Foundation

When The Robert Wood Johnson Foundation (RWJF) needed to increase transparency and insights around their grant-making efforts we knew a revamped Grants Explorer page would help. We decided to lead with an “at-a-glance” look into high-level, grant-based data along with a new approach for browsing and viewing grants (past and present).

Past some context-setting a grant-seeker could toggle between various data-visualizations including amounts awarded over time by category and focus area or by a portfolio distribution displaying a breakdown by topic and category. Each visualization would give valuable insights into RWJF’s generous and rich grant-making efforts.

Wireframes & designs for the Grants Explorer Landing Page.

It was important to keep key, top-level information highly readable and scannable yet also allow for a more detailed look into the grant itself. Displaying this information when the user chose to “open” a grant-record kept the performance efficient and still allowed further exploration such as the grant’s key-purpose, meta-data, contact information along with any related grant-specific resources, such as reports or outcome highlights.

Wireframes & designs for the Grantee Detail pages along with the How We Work entry page.

While the primary focus was on browsing and search the grants database, we took the opportunity to revamp and refresh the Grantee Detail pages as well. With a focus on presenting the Grantee’s organization, contact detail, and a call-to-action to get-involved, user could also view any impact success stories and/or browse a particular RWJF outcome measure such as Research & Evaluation Reports or similar Grant History.

By combining data-visualizations, high-performant grant searching, and more informative and action-oriented Grantee pages The Robert Wood Johnson Foundation was able to help surface more information, more quickly all while placing a special spotlight on their Grantees’ success through the grant-making process.
